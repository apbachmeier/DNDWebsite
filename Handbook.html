Introduction

Dungeons and Dragons (also known as D&D) is a tabletop role-playing game that has been around for decades. Dungeon Master (DM) typically acts as a narrator for a party of adventurers.

Chapter 1: Step-by-Step Characters

There are all kinds of different possibilites for creating a Dungeons and Dragons character. Build the kind of character you want to role-play as by following the steps below.

  1. Choose a Race
  2. Choose a Class
  3. Determine Your Ability Scores
  4. Describe Your Character
  5. Choose Equipment
  6. Come Together

Ability Scores

  • Strength
  • Dexterity

  • Intelligence
  • Wisdom
  • Charisma

Chapter 2: Races

Choosing a race is the fist step in creating your adventurer. There are nine different races outlined in the players handbook of the 5e Dungeons and Dragons. Each of these races have different traits. These traits can range from ability increases to languages to other abilities such as darkvision. Some of the more popular/common races have subraces to give even more variety which are outlined under there corresponding races below.

Dwarf

Dwarfs are a short and sturdy people who live long and

Dwarf Traits:

Ability Score Increase: Constitution +2
Age: Mature at the same rate as humans. Live to be 350 on average
Alignment: Generally Lawful, tend towards good as well.
Size: 4-5 ft. tall, ~150 lbs. size = medium
Speed: 25ft.
Darkvision: You can see in dim light as if it was bright light for 60 ft and in darkness as if it was dim light.
Dwarven Resilience: Gain advantage on all saving throws against poison, and resistance to poison damage.
Dwarven Combat Training: Gain proficiency with the battleaxe, handaxe, throwing hammer, and warhammer
Tool Proficiencies: Gain Proficiency with either smith's tools, brewers supplies, or mason's tools
Stonecunning: Gain Proficiency bonus in history checks related to the origin of stonework, double your proficiency bonus when you add to these checks
Languages: Commmon, Dwarvish

Dwarven Subraces

Hill Dwarf:
Ability Score Increase: Wisdom +1
Dwarven Toughness: Gain +1 maximum hit point per level

Mountain Dwarf:
Ability Score Increase: Strength +2
Dwarven Armor Training Proficiency with light and medium armor

Elf

Elves can live to be over 700 years old. This gives them a different perspective on events that may impact humans and other races with shorter lifespans more. Elves tend to be a bit shorter than humans and have slim, fine features.

Elf Traits:

Ability Score Increase: Dexterity +2
Age: 700+ years
Alignment: Often chaotic good due to a strong emphasis on freedom for themselves and others. Drow tend to be of an evil alignment though
Size: 5-6 ft. tall, medium size, slender build
Speed: 30 ft.
Darkvision: You can see in dim light as if it was bright light for 60 ft and in darkness as if it was dim light.
Keen Senses: Gain proficiency in Perception skill
Fey Ancestry: You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed and magic cannot put you to sleep
Trance: Elves don't need to sleep. Instead they go into deep meditation for 4 hours
Languages: Common, Elvish

Elven Subraces

High Elf:
Ability Score Increase: Intelligence +1
Elf Weapon Training: Gain Proficiency with longsword, shortsword, shortbow, and longbow
Cantrip: You know one cantrip from the wizards spell list, Intelligence is your spellcasting ability
Extra Language: You know one more language

Wood Elf:
Ability Score Increase: Wisdom +1
Elf Weapon Training: Gain Proficiency with longsword, shortsword, shortbow, and longbow
Fleet of Foot: Base movement speed is 35 ft.
Mask of the Wild: you can attempt to hide even when only lightly obscured in a natural area

Dark Elf(Drow):
Ability Score Increase: Charisma +1
Superior Darkvision: Darkvision increased to 120 ft. with the same conditions for standard darkvision.
Sunlight Sensitivity: You have disadvantage on attack and perception rolls that rely on sight when you, the target you are trying to attack, the item you are trying to percieve is in direct sunlight.
Drow Magic: You know the dancing lights cantrip, at 3rd level you can cast faerie fire once/day, at 5th level you can cast darkness once/day. Charisma is your spellcasting ability.

Dragonborn

This is some seriously messed up stuff. Just think about the implications that are being portrayed by what this race is...

Dragonborn Traits:

Ability Score Increase: Strength +2, Charisma +1

Size: 6+ ft. tall, 250lbs. Medium size
Speed: 30 ft.

Breath Weapon: You can use your breath to deal damage equal to 2d6 on a failed save and half damage on a successful save. DC = 8 + Constitution Modifier + Proficiency bonus. Damage type is based on draconic ancestry. Damage scales 3d6 at 6th level, 4d6 at 11th level, and 5d6 at 16th level
Damage Resistance: You have damage resistance to the type of damage associated with your draconic ancestry
Languages: Common, Draconic

Chapter 3: Classes

Classes are the second step of creating your adventurer. The class that you choose to specialize in is what determines most of the features and abilities that your character is able to perfrom. There are 12 different classes outlined in the Player's Handbook.

Barbarian

Fierce warriors of primitive background who can enter a battle rage.

Class Features:

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d12 per barbarian level
Hit Points at 1st level: 12 + constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d12(or 7) + your consitution modifier per barbarian level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: Light armor, medium armor, shields
Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons
Tools: None
Saving Throws: Strength, Constitution
Skills: Choose two from Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, and Survival

Equipment

Choose from the equipment below in addition to your background equipment.

  • a) A greataxe or b) any martial melee weapon

Chapter 4: Personality and Backgrounds

This is where a large part of how your role-playing will come into effect. Your race and class have an influence on how the world may view or interact with your character but creating a personality and background for your character really open up options to make a character memorable and interesting to interact with.

Your character traits can be just as meaningful as your race or class if it is something that you want to emphasis in your role play. alternatively you can choose something very standard and disregard these smaller nuances altogether. Some of these traits outlined in the players handbook include.

  • Name
  • Sex
  • Height and Weight
  • Alignment
  • Languages
  • Personal Characteristics
    • Personality Traits
    • Ideals
    • Bond
    • Flaws

Backgrounds

Each backgrounds gives 2 skill proficiencies and an additional feature simialar to a class or racial ability. Some backgrounds give tool or language proficiencies as well. The players handbook also outlines optional Personality Traits, Ideals, Bonds, and Flaws that you can role for if you need some inspiration.

Acolyte

Skill Proficiencies: Insight, Religion
Languages: Two of your choice
Equipment: Holy symbol, a prayer book or prayer wheel, 5 sticks of incense, vestments, a set of common clothes, 15 gp
Feature: Shelter of the Faithful- Fellow worshippers of your diety or religion will give you and your party free healing and care at a temple or shire of your faith.

Charlatan

Skill Proficiencies: Deception, Sleight of Hand
Tool Proficiencies: Disguise kit, Forgery kit
Equipment: Fine clothes, Disguise kit, tool of a con of your choice (ten sotppered bottles filled with colored liquid, a set of weighted dice, a deck of marked cards, or a signet ring of an imaginary duke) 15 gp.
Feature: False Identity- You have a second identity with documents, acquaintances and disguises that allow you to assume that persona. You can also forge documents including official papers and personal letters so long as you have seen an example of the kind of document or the handwriting you are trying to copy.

Criminal

Skill Proficiencies: Deception, Stealth
One type of gaming set, thieves' tools
Equipment: a crowbar, set of dark common clothes with a hood, 15 gp.
Feature: Criminal Contact- you have a contact within a network of other criminals typically related ot your criminal specialty. You can get messages to your contact at pretty much any time you want through local contacts that are also involved in criminal activities.

Entertainer

Skill Proficiencies: Acrobatics, Performance
Tool Proficiencies: Disguise kit, one type of musical instument
Equipment: musical instument (of your choice), a favor of an admirer (love letter, lock of hair, or trinket), a costume, 15 gp
Feature: By Popular Demand- You can get free lodging and food if you perform at a venue each night. Strangers recognize you in town where you have performed and typically like you.

Folk Hero

Skill Proficiencies: Animal Handling, Survival
Tool Proficiencies: One type of artisan's tools, vehicles(land)
Equipment: Artisan's tools, shovel, iron pot, set of common clothes, 10 gp.
Feature: Rustic Hospitality- You can find a place to hide, rest or recuperate with commoners unless you are a danger to them.
